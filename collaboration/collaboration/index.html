Collaboration

Teamtools Studio welcomes and encourages collaboration from any party that thinks they can make use of our software. This includes private, government, and educational entities.

While our software was originally written for a spaceflight context, we believe strongly that it could be used for any complex system operations, so long as you buy into enough of our core concepts (like mimicking an AMPCS dictionary for commands and telemetry metadata).

This section should give you the tools you need in order to succeed in collaborating with us or even just extending our work into your own.

We have made many large and small choices throughout the codebase, but have provided tts_starter_template as a way for you to bootstrap yourself into these standards when you create a new library. We recommend that you start there for a completely new library, or by the appropraite DemoSat adaptation library as a tempalte if you are starting your own adaptation.

Much of our collaboration model comes from the NASA AMMOS SLIM project, but has been tailored to this project where the SLIM baseline does not effectively serve our needs.

In general we believe that the best path forward is to share the philosophies of those at NASA who have come before us, but we also recognize that no reference baseline will accurately reflect all projects using it, and tailoring is always needed.
